# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in this educational project welcoming, respectful, and useful for learners and contributors of every background and experience level.

## Expected behaviour

Participants should:

- communicate with patience and respect;
- give constructive, specific, and technically grounded feedback;
- welcome questions from beginners;
- acknowledge sources and original authors;
- protect private, confidential, and regulated data;
- focus discussion on improving the project and helping learners.

## Unacceptable behaviour

The following are not acceptable:

- harassment, discrimination, insults, or personal attacks;
- publishing another person's private information;
- submitting confidential datasets, credentials, or access tokens;
- plagiarism or deliberate misrepresentation of benchmark results;
- disruptive, deceptive, or persistently off-topic behaviour.

## Educational integrity

Contributors must not present synthetic or illustrative benchmark results as production evidence. Dataset provenance, preprocessing, evaluation assumptions, limitations, and known uncertainty should be documented clearly.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove comments, issues, pull requests, code, and other contributions that do not follow this code. Repeated or serious violations may lead to temporary or permanent exclusion from project spaces.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository maintainer. Do not include sensitive personal information in a public issue.

## Attribution

This policy is inspired by widely used open-source community conduct standards and adapted for an educational data-science repository.
